D226-280 09-06-19 - NOT Nazca lines. Old campsite drainage.
Along the way we pass this view of the campsite we've just left behind. At first I think this looks like some local version of Nazca lines. But the inverted "V"-shaped features are drainage channels between tenting areas. The idea is to make sure that water from the hillside above drains away from the tents and into the little creek at the bottom.

D226-281 09-06-19 - Looking back. A long ascent.
The altitude is beginning to have its effect. We're rising steeply and steadily now. A few hikers are visible on the path to the right.

D226-282 09-06-19 - Stairway to heaven.
These stairs are continuous from here on. Derek admits he has to take ten steps and stop for breath, then repeat this sequence all the way to the top. I, of course, as your disembodied narrator, am feeling quite fresh and full of vigour. Now where are those coca leaves. My chewing wad needs a little refreshment.

D226-283 09-06-19 - The top of the pass in sight.
The lucky ones who've already reached the summit wait on that little knoll on the right.

D226-284 09-06-19 - Ramparts, no detour possible.
On either side of the trail, the highest peaks rake moisture out of the passing clouds. The turf is thick and probably brilliant green in season. No brush or trees survive at this altitude.

D226-285 09-06-19 - Warmiwanusca Pass, 4200m.
Finally a good view of the top. This is the Warmiwanusa pass, 4200 metres. The name means Dead Woman Pass in Quechua.

D226-286 09-06-19 - We toil upward.
Derek begins to feel like he too might become a Peruvian place name. Don't know if they'd rename this the Dead Guy Pass just for him. Ah, there's three of our group. Franka, Merle and Angelo the guide coming up.

D226-289 09-06-19 - Resting at the top.
Derek finally reaches the summit along with the other hikers now catching their breath. Angelo on the left waits for the rearguard. The weather goes suddenly cold. Wind sluices up the pass from behind us and down this side. It begins to rain and sleet.

D226-290 09-06-19 - Pelted with sleet.
As the squall moves over the pass, it passes down to the valley below, creating a rainbow.

D226-291 09-06-19 - Rainbow over the valley.
A telephoto view. The campsite with the Nazca lines is halfway down. Farm fields are visible at the very bottom.

D226-295 09-06-19 - Looks so much easier in retrospect.
Looking back, as the weather passes over and the clouds part. The smile of the Sun-god works wonders to dispel fatigue.

D226-297 09-06-19 - The other side of the pass.

D226-313 09-06-19 - Thirsty wayfarer, slaking.
From another group. The trail stretches ahead for miles and miles. This is part of the joy of high altitude hiking. The ability to see far ahead and to anticipate a route. It's not an ordinary experience for most of us. We go through our days beset by the close details of life, without any far-reaching vision of where we're going, or why.

That's our trail going all the way down toward the right side of the picture.

d230-167 09-06-20 - Carole D: Our last lunch on the trail.
The porters go to all the trouble of setting up a tent with tables and chairs for lunch. Remember, this is not a fixed campsite with road access. Porter people have had to carry all the equipment, cooking utensils and fuel, plus the food itself, from our starting point.

D226-359 09-06-20 - Flute player with another group.

D226-379A 09-06-20 - Atmospheric environment FX
Here the details of foliage are reduced to Turner-esque glints of sunlight on the leaves. The finer detail of the distant mountains has been blended with a wet-on-wet process, but the outlines are preserved even in the clouds. These have an agressive quality that harmonizes with a kind of "hob-knobbing with the elements" idea.
Real figures, iconic landscape. The original details of the figures have been allowed to show through. The digital treatment can be a composite of different effects. You can combine various steps of abstraction and interpretation with the original image, and apply the result only to local areas of that image. Thus, the clouds are softly blended within, but outlined more clearly along the edges.
