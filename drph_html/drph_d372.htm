2015-09-23 Around home
* Actually devouring the apples, contrary to the ladybug's carnivorous, fellow bug-eating reputation.
* As in, Secret Storm on the Edge of Night General Hospital -- the soap opera. That blue thing beyond the woodpile is the remains of an IKEA shelf unit. Deemed too useless and too awful to let live. Condemned since then to be a floor unit for the compost heap. Our cast-iron "krater" peeks out on the left from a tangle of creeper vines. My conceit is to compare it to an overwrought classical specimen known as the Derveni Krater and found in Macedonia -- a relic of late flourishing artistic inspiration around the time of Alexander. And before the neighbours arrived to focus everyone's mind on "peace, order and good (Roman) government."
* that makes me want to preserve it for at least part of eternity. ..
* Preciosity? A writ-guru I once had, opined that preciosity was loving something more than God does. But this presents a theological problem. If God is all-wise, all-knowing and all-good, then He is not selfish and does NOT love Himself, right? So then, to avoid preciosity, I should not love God more than He loves Himself. In fact, be indifferent or at least slightly dislike the guy. ..
* So, either compost or combust in senior-common room hell, writ-crit guru. God is watching. In fact God's hand is afoot, and you're gonna get the divine boot.
