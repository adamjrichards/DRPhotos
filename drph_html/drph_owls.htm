owls -
These scans were made with an early black and white hand-scanner. Same time period as the Temple Tikvah bulletin. Oddly, most of the pictures were on a kitchen hot pad. The artist or owner of the copyright is unknown.
The Owl Show is Derek's first purely digital slide show. Other early slide shows originated from 35mm slide projector collections.

OWL1 - Barred Owl
The Barred owl is forever debarred from polite society. No one knows why. And the owl is prudently saying nothing.

OWL10 - Temperance Owl
This Temperance owl is guarding an empty glass -- misguidedly. One might almost say, intemperately.

OWL11 - Bon-vivant Owl
The Bon-vivant owl has had a few too many.

OWL3 - Long-eared Owl
The Long-eared owl has a knack for overhearing gossip.

OWL4 - Great Grey Owl
The Great Grey owl is indeed, both great and gray ...

OWL2 - Great Grey Owl, surprised
but surprised that you should take the time to comment on something so obvious.

OWL5 - Owl of Athena
The Owl of Athena is worried that you may find out the secret of its wisdom ...

OWL5R - Owl of Athena, reversed
which is, that it has eyes, like the god Janus, on both sides of its head.

OWL7 - Predatory Owl
The Predatory owl thinks that a mouse dinner would be a real hoot.

OWL8 - Inebriated Owl
The Inebriated owl is a close relative of the Bon-vivant owl, but has given up all hope of respectability.

OWL9 - Bump-on-a-log Owl
The Bump-on-a-log owl gets a reputation for wisdom by merely looking confused and saying very little -- wisely.

End of the Owl Show.
