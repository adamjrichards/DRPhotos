1974-05 - Grimsby to St. Catharines. This was our first serious family walking project. We all took the train to Grimsby, then set off west for St. Catharines. The project turned out more arduous than we'd expected. Nathan and I were the only ones to finish. The others bailed out at Ball's Falls or someplace in the middle of the route, due to foot problems. They were able to catch a bus back to St. Catharines and home.
We had tents and carried a stove and food supply. The kids had improvised bedrolls rather than sleeping bags, but the weather was warm. In those days, camping in designated areas, such as Cave Springs and Ball's Falls, was allowed. Lacking a convenient campsite when nearing St. Catharines, Nathan and I had to pitch our tent in the woods, just off the trail.
