Fresh Air: St. Mark’s Summit, a hike with heavenly views

St. Mark's Summit on Vancouver's North Shore is a nice, moderately challenging hike that's less crowded than the Grouse Grind and offers views of Howe Sound.

By: Graeme McRanor Metro Published on

There’s no shortage of hikes with glorious views on the North Shore - but a real nice, moderately challenging one that’s less crowded than the overrun and oft-obnoxious Grouse Grind™ is the climb to St. Mark’s Summit in Cypress Provincial Park.

Yes, it takes a little longer to get there. And, yes, it’s more of a time commitment once you’ve arrived. But glory day - there’s no gaggles of tourists or neon-clad competitive climbers to avoid - and you don’t have to pay to get down.

Also, it’s not trademarked, to the best of my knowledge.

  • Related: Fresh Air: Panorama Ridge, an epic hike that lives up to its name
  • Related: Fresh Air: Hiking this summer? Here’s what you need to know

It’s also kid-friendly, if said kid doesn’t mind climbing a mountain. My seven-year-old minds, but he’ll summit for incentives (bribes/ice cream).

To find the trailhead, park in the Cypress ski lot and walk north towards the chairlift - you’re looking for Howe Sound Crest Trail signage.

Once on the trail, follow it uphill onto a wider ski run and keep left towards the green water tower. Go right onto the trail just before tower and head uphill through the trees until you hit a gravel road. Go left - the road soon narrows and becomes the Howe Sound Crest Trail, which you’ll follow until you get to the top. There are certainly some steep switchback sections to navigate - and on sunny summer weekends you’ll definitely come across plenty of other hikers - so exercise caution (after rain, roots and rocks can be slippery), particularly at the lookout point at the top, which can get relatively crowded with people taking photos and is more or less a sheer drop to Lions Bay, a few thousand feet below.

After descending back to the parking lot, stop in at the Crazy Raven Bar & Grill for some munchies and a cold beer - you’ve earned it.

TRAIL DEETS

Difficulty: Intermediate

Time: 5 hours return

Distance: 11 kilometres return

Elevation gain: 460 metres over 5.5 kilometres.

HOW TO GET THERE (Driving time: approximately 40 minutes from Vancouver)

Head up to Cypress Provincial Park on the North Shore and park in the ski lot. Public transit: No.
