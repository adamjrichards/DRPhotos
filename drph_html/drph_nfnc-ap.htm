D196-168 07-09-07 - Searching for the mansion ruins.

D196-173 07-09-07 - The front steps?
How did this tree, which is probably older than the ruins get planted right in front of these steps? They must have led straight into the house. So it's a puzzle how a tree, that looks older that the ruin, could have been located exactly in the path of the people who would use that step.

D196-174 07-09-07 - An old tree here doesn't make sense.

D196-175 07-09-07 - The privy?

AL03-014B V08-12-21 - Cliff's garden, early vegetarian period.
Left to right: Enid, Scott. Derek (on the ground), Lucy Hinton, Fraser Cliff, Wendy Cliff (on the ground), Albert Hinton (The Rev), Muriel Cliff. Eric Richards is taking the picture. Muriel Cliff was my mother, Enid's, sister.

This is a throwback to our days living in Quebec. We Richardses, being renters all our lives, were often a bit amused and bemused by the Cliff's gardening preoccupations. Our own gardens were always the strictly vegetarian allotments of apartment dwellers who fail to see the point of beautifying someone else's property. This is taken in the Cliff's garden behind the small apartment building on Westmore Ave in Montreal where they lived in the late 40s before moving to Pointe Claire.

0366-34 92-06 - Cliff's garden, later aesthetic era.
Perhaps we Richardses were a tad jealous of relatives who could garden for purely aesthetic reasons.

D196-357p - Panorama from The Lookout.
I mean the desire to recreate in miniature what Nature does splendidly on a large scale. Below us is a garden of gently rolling forest that stretches to the limit of vision. This is decorated with a few small lakes and the rocks we stand on. The utter neatness of it leaves you feeling pleasantly stunned by the groomed appearance of all those tree-tops.
