2011-07 Whirlpool - Wintergreen Flats
At 2pm, getting restless. Not tired enough to nap. Not energetic enough to tackle home improvement projects or gardening chores. The childhood urge to explore asserts itself.

This means rotating through a list of all the places we usually go for an outing. Niagara Gorge and the Whirlpool seems to be the only one not visited recently. But after my recent incarceration at the General for E-coli, these seem questionable.

I promise myself to be CAREFUL, and to pay close attention to that inner control panel. This has various dials and readouts indicating body temperature, respiration rate, heart rate, lactose in the legs, plus a fuzzy generalized indicator of depletion rate and remaining reserves.

The downhill steps to the Whirlpool area are numerous but easy. At the bottom the trail becomes much more challenging. Several small landslips have obliterated sections of trail and a few fallen trees need to be removed.

Past the point, beneath the outer end of the Aero Car, the trail is dry but constantly up and down. Here's where the energy reserves begin to get down. On the upgrade again at the Wintergreen Flats end, I have to husband my energy by resting often. Reminds me of the Inca Trail, only this is practically sea-level, not at 3,000m altitude.

Home again 5:30. That's about 2:50 of walking, 40min driving.

2011-07-17 Sunday At the beach, Port Dover.
An excursion with Miriam, Adoni and Adam. Adam is here to look after Diana and Danny's place in Ridgeway. At Port Dover we park on the east side of the harbour and walk across the bridge into town. The streets are packed with weekenders.

Straight to the beach-side resto for fish and chips. We snag a prime table on the balcony overlooking the beach. Dense, end-to-end, congestion, packed with bods and brollies. Cheerful server reassures us that the feathery palm trees, planted beside the balcony, won't freeze when the cold weather comes. They'll go into a nice warm greenhouse for the winter.

After the meal, we claim a few square meters of sand, strip down to undergarment bathing suits and head for the water. Warm, modest waves. Once immersed, I try out Miriam's waterproof camera. Can't see the screen in the bright sun. Can't even tell if it is off or on. What do you make of a fun-at-the-beach camera with a screen you can only see indoors?

I stroll the beach looking for pics. A sand sculpture commemorates the NASA space shuttle. Hordes of teen-age girls parade in two-piece suits. Cute, but not a deeply moving experience. Grasshopper figures with, one must admit, hints of future bodaciousness. But not yet. This really is a people's beach. The elite, with impressive bods, go elsewhere to show off.

Icecream from a booth on the pier. We take a walk out to the lighthouse, then drive home via Wellandport and Welland.
