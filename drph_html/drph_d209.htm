2008-07-07 To the Chippewa Reserve for presentation of the report Merle had consulted on. I drive off to look for the tile-ditching machine I'd heard about on a previous trip. Reach the Harris farm, as suggested, but Harris informs me that he doesn't own such a machine. Eventually extract from him the information that a certain guy, two stop signs down Irish Road, has it. The machine was only contracted briefly to Harris. From the tone of his voice I interpret subtle reservations about the owner of the machine.

Driving down Irish Road, can't find the second stop sign, as advised, but do pass a Red Green-esque dirt farm with a yardful of decrepit machinery. No sign of the tile-ditching machine I've been hunting. The farmer and family are kicking back on the front porch sipping tall cool ones. Yes, he does have a tile-ditching machine, down the lane on his back 40 acreage. Why do I want to know? I set out, after getting permission to trespass and take pictures. Behind me a tractor starts up with a roar and the farmer comes up from behind to give me a ride on his haywagon along with his grandaughter.

The tile-ditching machine is way out back on a trailor attached to a tractor. I cling to the sides of the wagon as we bump along a farm track. He parks and I dismount to take many snaps of the old machine with its characteristic bucketed wheel and "rudder" out the back.

Larry, the farmer, is a real character, sunburnt, cheerful as he shows me old trucks and cars that he might do something with someday. But he thinks that the doctor across the street is a snob, and that the natives are ... well. His own house and barn are almost as pictureque as the machinery collection. I gather he lives by marginal farming, rural odd jobs, and ditch digging projects. Despite his cheerfulness, he is probably conscious that his neighbours regard him as an unforgettable character, but low caste.

I take down his name and address and promise to send copies of the pictures, then depart across a barnyardful of wandering muscovy ducks, a batch of recently born kittens and a barking dog. I took great care not to run over any critter while pulling away.
