D172-125 07-01-20 - Brock walk, artistic.

D172-125A - The straight goods.
This struck me as an inherently "artistic" subject. Suitable for PhotoShop watercolourization or something similar. The "S" pattern of the creek, the cold blue of the water, the simplicity of it. It might have that elusive staying power, a quiet evocativeness, that a more dramatic, emotive or active image sometimes lacks. Wow! We're on the edge of aesthetic theory here. The difference between an image that repeatedly offers an imaginative departure, a nuanced recognition, rather than asserting a more specific information content.
Brock student residences visible through the trees.
This is also a case where I quickly recognised the structure of the image in the field, framed it carefully, including and excluding elements. My equipment doesn't make this easy. Viewfinder and viewscreen are not well adapted to contemplating a composition, especially under stressful environmental conditions. Unfortunately an SLR, while providing better previewing would be far more difficult to lug around. So, at present, I basically have to preview the composition in my head, then take a wide enough picture to include extra space, allowing for likely horizon tilt, inability to see the exact edge of the frame, etc.
