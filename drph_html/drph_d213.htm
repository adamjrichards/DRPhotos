Aug 9, Saturday Rain again. We're off on the studio tour. We gather insights into what people will buy as art at the low end of the cost spectrum. It has to have a hand-made, souvenir quality and be cheap. So much for sensitive landscape photos.
After lunch in Wilberforce, rain continuing, we stop at the county fair and slurp our way across sodden fields to dripping exhibition booths. After briefly ogling heavy horse pulling and lady blacksmithing, we head back to the cottage to drown (no, dry out) our sorrows.

2008-08-07 - Paddling Lake Contau.

Aug 10, Sunday Dawn dawns. David and Debbie are here. A ray of sunshine penetrates the clouds. We live in hope. I do a kayak assembly demonstration for David on the dock. The two of us paddle down to the dam and back. A brief rain shower. In the afternoon I paddle Merle to the other end of the lake. (The grand old Duke of York?) The new Thermarest canoe seats are a success. I also try out my new wetsuit and socks at the dock: a success, very comfortable. Also add buoyancy when immersed up to the gills.

D213-027 08-08-11 - Merle's rock.

Aug 11, Monday Overcast with blue holes. I solo kayak to the upper end of the lake. And sit in a waterlily meadow considering what a comfortable platform this kayak is for the waterway flaneur. A heightened sense of the fine details of local existence. Sounds: distant traffic, nearer, a frog glug-glugging, a duck paddling among the lilies. Closer still, a single drip from my paddle sounds a loud plop. The boat moves a few inches at a time as the breeze shifts.

While the kayak float, me absolutely still, a slight tremble in the water radiates outward from the hull. I try a light tap on the gunwale with one finger, another train of ripples. I suspect that my heartbeat is actually contributing to the ripples. A creature in the water with sensitive enough hearing could check my pulse.

To Haliburton after lunch. Shopping, a walk in the park. A very pleasant, clear river flows through the town and into the lake. Kids are jumping off the bank and swimming.
