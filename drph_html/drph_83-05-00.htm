May/83

With the Ames Expedition, late June, 1816 - Approaching the Turtle Mountains

He leaned back in the saddle and stared upward. A black disk of cloud, swirling at the edges, hung over them, its top piled miles high with nimbus. A tongue of fire licked down from the centre and was gone in a flicker, its prey a cluster of trees twenty yards to the right. A sapling toppled over emitting a cloud of steam. Infinitesimally delayed, the impact of the sound exploded from the inside of his skull to echo off the dark roof of cloud.

"Near one, that," remarked Sergeant Crocker, reaching inside the lapel of his coat for tobacco.

A bird warbled in a bush nearby; the branch to which it clung swayed in the breeze. The vast shadow passed over and the sun shone down as before.
