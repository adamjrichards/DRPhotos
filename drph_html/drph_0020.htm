70-10-00 First 35mm, St. Clair

The Year 1970
* Moved to 72 Major St. 1967.
* Derek's 1st job in Toronto, 1969.
* D's first 35mm camera.
St. Clair and Yonge area. My first job in Toronto. First film with Practica camera. October 1970. Camera was bought second hand at Black's Camera, just around the corner from the office. This was a departure from my earlier reliance on the Solida 120 format camera bought in Germany in the 1950s. I had backed the wrong format for too long. 35mm was really more convenient in many ways. More pictures per film roll. Convenient colour slide format with numerous projectors and other accessories available.
This dead-end, low-level, essentially clerical job was in some ways a highlight of my employment career. The staff got along well. Noon hours were a delight with shop windows to look in, parks to sit in and read a book. This is where I first met Mark Brett and Linda Hankin. Mark introduced me to Graham Greene's novels and these became a favourite. I used to take my lunch to the reservoir park, shown in some of this pictures, and read for half an hour (when I wasn't taking pictures.
The ofice was eventually moved downtown to Bay St. in the Queen's Park area. This also had superlative lunch time loitering features.
