D227-218 09-06-24 - Festival day, Avenido del Sol.

June 24 Cuzco, Inti Raymi Festival.

D227-250 09-06-24 - Officialdom, waiting for the Inca.
Something iconic about this tableau: civil servants, mandarins, chamberlains bowing and scraping. In the ante-rooms of power, but making all the real decisions. Second from the left, he has an especially cunning look. Something like Sir Humphrey in the Britcom "Yes, Minister."

D227-253 09-06-24 - Himself.
The Inca himself appears. An arresting figure. Our guide says that the role is assigned to an actor who has dark skin, a big nose, a booming voice and who is fluent in Quechua, the language of the Incas and rural Peru.

D227-277 09-06-24 - Next, to the Plaza d'Armas.
We change location to the Plaza d'Armas nearby. Here the festivities take on a processional character. Inca style music maintains a steady, heart-pounding thump-thump. Interrupted sometimes by glitches in the sound system.

June 24 Sacsayhuaman, Inti Raymi Fest.

D227-312 09-06-24 -
We're delivered to Sacsayhuaman, the main archaelogical site of Cuzco, and the location of the main Inti Raymi pageant. The less privileged coat the top of the hill like a swarm of bees. A hundred thousand, suggested our guide.

D227-306 09-06-24 - Looking for our bleachers seats.
The bleachers are reserved for ticket holders. We, thanks to Metropolitan tours, are ticket-holders. Also holders of a fine box lunch.

D227-316 09-06-24 - Followed by the higher-ups.
At the top the bank of ancient stones, the Inca leads a retinue of his officers of state.

D227-319 09-06-24 - And maidens.
No ceremony is complete without the maiden element. Up against the far wall we see a team of green-visored helpers. And various papparazi with cameras on tripods. Not present in the original Inca ceremonial.

D227-321 09-06-24 -
These appear to be the same straight-backed ladies of an earlier stage of the pageant, back in Cuzco town. Note the interesting contrast of the orange ladies against the grey rocks.

D227-322 09-06-24 - J.C. is not amused.
We wonder how much this festival represents present day belief. The crowd is nominaly Catholic. But the fervour of the event is more than merely folkloric pageant. The statue of J.C. was donated by Palestinian Christians.

D227-359 09-06-24 - Spanish moss people?

D227-352 09-06-24 - At last the Inca calls on higher powers.
On a central dais, built to look like the surrounding rock, the Inca makes his pitch for a good year. Although this is June 24, for reasons of civic convenience, this represents the solstice, in effect, an equatorial New Year.
