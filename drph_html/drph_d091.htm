Note the transition in the above from an attempt that leans toward specifing or declaring the subject, to a subsequent shakier but more descriptive approach. Somewhere before the summer of '94, in Salt Lake City, I must have been reading about "contour drawing". It seemed a minor revelation at the time that you can produce an amusing and faithful picture, not by some kind of foreknowlege or inherent skill, but simply by using very basic powers of observation. By an affectionate attention to the appearance of the subject.
