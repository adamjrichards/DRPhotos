See those 5 small stones embedded in the rock wall? Steps to permit Inca workers to get from one terrace level to the next. We've already used similar steps in other parts of the site. This detail brings home our common mentality. I've just made a resolution to never again bad-mouth the "stone age". As if this was a period full of flintstone-like, knuckle-dragging primitives.

Notice the pattern of steps on the right ridge. Looks almost as if it was created deliberately. Probably an accidental pattern. But it does seems to have echoes in Inca iconography like the scalloped edges in some of the agricultural terraces we saw a few days ago.

D226-536 09-06-22 - Meanwhile, back on the Orient Express.
Way back there in a telephoto view we see a train of the Machu Picchu RR, owned by the Orient Express. We heard our guides complain about this company for reducing service for the locals and treating the line as a cash cow for the tourist trade.

D226-539 09-06-22 -
These walls look almost too fresh and new to have survived from the 16th century. If they have been restored, the stones have certainly been placed with loving care. It looks almost like a contemporary building project in progress, only waiting for the roofs and interiors to be finished.

D226-540 09-06-22 - Do I see an echo?
Several stones like this on the site, reflecting nearby mountain profiles. The Incas not only coped with their environment, they seemed to worship the mountains. Something in common with a few people today, I'll hazard.

D226-542 09-06-22 -
Another example of a large stone mini-mountain embedded in other construction. They seemed to have a genius for making an organic whole out of their own construction and the rocky features of the site.

D226-543 09-06-22 - Serious stone-work
A fine example of Inca masonry. Hard to fit a knife blade between many of these stones. How did the Incas do this? I don't think I've heard a definitive answer. Probably not just a genius for predicting how a stone would fit, then somehow carving it ahead of time, and having it miraculously TURN OUT to fit. I suspect they had a practical trial-and-error method.
For instance: trying the fit, with, say, a layer of leaves in between, removing the stone, seeing the discrepancy printed in green on the rock surfaces, taking material off to improve the fit, repeating this to get as close as desired. My dentist uses this technique with some sort of "dental carbon paper" which I have to bite on. In addition, the stones could be roughly dished where they meet. If one face was dished more than its corresonding face, then the stones would meet only along a narrower outer edge. This could then be speedily trimmed from the outside without completely and repeatedly removing the stone from its position.

D226-544 09-06-22 - We ascend the high altar.
The crowd has thinned out overnight, but still a line-up. This is probably one of the most popular destinations on the site, next to the guard house.

D226-548 09-06-22 - A gnomon and calendar stone.
The focal point of the high altar. The planes of the stone appear to be oriented either with points of the Inca compass, dates in their calendar, or with other locations within view on the horizon.

D226-549 09-06-22 - Feel the power?
Hard to get near the stone, particularly with that tape round the edge to keep us from putting paws on it. An enterprising guide is trying to suggest to this young group that it may radiate a mysterious force. He has everybody close their eyes and hold out palms to feel for the emanations emitted by this rock of power.

D226-549A V10-07-12 - Feel the power? FX
The visitors' faces have been allowed to show through the outline effect. The picture is also brighter, contrastier and sharper than the original. But it dispenses with a small amount of (hopefully irrelevant) detail. Generally, the outline effect lends itself more easily to subjects or artifacts that have close to regular shapes, such as, architecture, masonry, horizons, clothing.

D226-552 09-06-22 -
I don't feel any emanations, but certainly do feel the significance of the thing. It's a focal point of such a vast system of terraces and buildings, of a trail traversed for hundreds of years by runners, porters and pilgrims up to the present day. It would be hard NOT to be focussed on this singular object, even if you don't know its exact cultural significance

D226-553 09-06-22 - Slightly tapered window treatments.
On the high altar platform, Merle is standing in front of a typical Inca window. We're not quite sure if this taper was a purely aesthetic preference or a structural issue. The lintel stone could be smaller than with a purely rectangular window, and the form would approach a little closer to the ideal of an arch.

D226-556 09-06-22 - House walls repeat mountain profile.
Do these house walls look like the mountain profiles behind them? The Inca house-building technique was probably governed by the contruction materials at hand. Yet there's a certain consistency that could have reinforced the self-image of the inhabitants as a mountain people.

I could picture the wearer of that garden-party hat presiding at a genteel tea-pouring with watercress sandwiches on the side. But maybe I'm barking up the wrong culture.

My heart goes out to her. By the by, I think I can now feel those mysterious vibes from the high altar in the soles of my feet.
