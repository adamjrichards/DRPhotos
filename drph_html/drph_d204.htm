D204-013A - You called, Great Jupiter?
All Toronto was shocked and appalled by the appearance of a colossal statue of Mercury in BCE Place.

16-09-08: Joking in bed this morn on the sobject of the gods cancelling my time-travel visa, recalled the "Great Jupiter" remark from a Canadian Opera Company performance at Mt A about 1961 or 1962. With Jan Rubes and such calibre artists. A leotard-clad figure of Mercury prances onto the stage of Fawcett Hall, declaiming in a very portentous voice: "You called, Great Jupiter?" I can't recall the context of the sketch, which was a mishmash of operatic fragments, but it brought down the house.

More recently, I was imagining the possible comic implications of entrusting a self-important and scatter-brained Mercury with the task of delivering a "final notice" to a wily octogenarian. This could be big. On a par with Don Juan in Hamilton or that film-noir classic of Bergman's, The Seventh Seal,where the hero bargains with the Devil. Only to be parodied by Newfoundland comedians in that pre-This Hour ... TV series, CODCO.
